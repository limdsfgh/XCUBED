SHOOTING

1. Using Natural Light as the Main Element

Natural light was the foundation of this entire shoot. Rather than overpowering the scenery with artificial lighting, we embraced the softness and unpredictability of outdoor light.

What this adds to the shoot:

  • Soft highlights create gentle transitions that feel organic.
  • Shadows shift throughout the day, introducing subtle changes that make each frame unique.
  • Reflections and diffused light add calmness, especially when interacting with greenery or textured surfaces.

By allowing the light to guide the compositions, each image carries a sense of honesty and natural mood.

2. Exploring Angles and Composition

A large part of the process involved experimenting with different perspectives.The goal was not just to document the environment, but to reinterpret it through framing.

What we focused on:

  • Low-angle shots to highlight textures in grass, soil, or natural structures.
  • Wide compositions to capture open spaces and the flow of the environment.
  • Negative space to create balance and a sense of quietness.
  • Lines and symmetry found naturally in the surroundings.

These choices help the visuals feel clean, intentional, and relaxing to look at.

3. Observing Small Details

Instead of focusing on a main subject, this series draws attention to the smaller elements around us.

Examples of details we emphasized:

  • The uneven pattern of leaves in soft wind
  • Subtle textures on tree bark or stone
  • How light falls across a pathway
  • The contrast between shadows and bright spots
  • Natural shapes formed by plants or branches

These details may seem simple, but they become powerful when framed thoughtfully. They form visual stories that feel grounded and real.

4. Color Palette and Mood

Color plays a huge role in shaping the emotional tone of the series.

Characteristics of the palette:

  • Soft greens and earth tones
  • Natural, unsaturated colors
  • Smooth transitions created by soft daylight
  • A calm, soothing overall atmosphere

We intentionally avoided heavy editing or overly vibrant colors. The aim was to maintain authenticity and preserve the peaceful mood found in the environment.
